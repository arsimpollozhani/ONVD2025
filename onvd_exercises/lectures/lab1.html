How to Survive Probability and Statistics

Ah yes, Probability and Statistics, the course where dreams go to die, and calculators get abused beyond reason. They say it's the hardest course, but honestly, that's just a fancy way of saying "it's a wild guessing game with extra math." But don't worry, young warrior, here’s a foolproof (or at least somewhat effective) guide to making it through.

Steps to Success (Or At Least Survival):

  1. Accept Your Fate
    • This is happening. You can cry about it later.
    • Probability says there’s a non-zero chance you’ll pass.
    • If ancient mathematicians did it without Google, so can you.
    • Embrace the chaos. It's the best way to get through.
    • After all, chaos is just an advanced form of probability.
    • It's like a random variable—you never know what you'll get, but you hope it's not a zero.
  2. Master the Art of Selective Attention
    • Not everything in class is important. Probably.
    • Pay attention to whatever the professor repeats. It's on the test.
    • Act like you understand when they ask questions. Nod wisely.
  3. Develop a Deep, Unhealthy Relationship with Your Calculator
    • Learn every function. Yes, even the weird ones.
    • Memorize shortcuts, or suffer.
    • - Give it a name. It’s your only friend now.
  4. Guess with Confidence
    • If you're unsure, pick the answer that sounds fancy.
    • When in doubt, say "normal distribution." People love that.
    • Use the power of educated guessing—it's like gambling, but legal.
